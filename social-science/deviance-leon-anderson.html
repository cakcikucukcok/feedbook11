Deviance: Social Constructions and Blurred Boundaries draws on up-to-date scholarship across a wide spectrum of deviance categories, providing a symbolic interactionist analysis of the deviance process. The book addresses positivistic theories of deviant behavior within a more encompassing description of the deviance process including the work of deviance claims-makers, rule-breakers, and social control agents. Students gain an understanding of the sociology of deviance through cross-cultural comparisons, historical description of deviance in the U.S. from colonial times to the present, and up-close analysis of the lived experience of those who are labeled deviant and responses to them in the U.S. today.
